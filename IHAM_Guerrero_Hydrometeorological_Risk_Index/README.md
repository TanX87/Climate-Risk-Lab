# IHAM: Actuarial Hydrometeorological Risk Index for Mexico

## Regional Climate Risk Assessment for the Insurance Sector — Guerrero, Mexico

## Overview

Climate change is increasing the frequency and severity of hydrometeorological events, creating new challenges for risk assessment in the insurance sector.

This project develops the **Índice Hidrometeorológico Actuarial de México (IHAM)**, a quantitative climate risk index designed to measure extreme hydrometeorological conditions in Guerrero, Mexico.

The objective is to integrate climate variability into an actuarial framework to support applications related to insurance risk management, exposure assessment, and climate risk analysis.

---

## Methodology

The model combines historical climate information from the **NASA POWER Climate Database (1985–2023)** with statistical and actuarial techniques.

The index incorporates four climate components:

- Extreme temperature frequency
- Maximum 5-day precipitation accumulation
- Extreme wind frequency
- Soil moisture saturation frequency

The methodology includes:

- Extreme event identification using empirical percentiles
- Climate indicator construction
- Regional standardization
- Multivariate weighting using CRITIC and MEREC methods

The analysis considers the regional climate heterogeneity of Guerrero through seven geographical regions.

---

## Key Findings

- Climate risk dynamics vary significantly across regions, making a single statewide indicator insufficient.
- Coastal areas show higher vulnerability during periods associated with extreme precipitation and tropical cyclone activity.
- The index successfully identifies increased climate stress during historical events such as Hurricane Paulina (1997) and Hurricane Patricia (2015).
- Regionalized climate indices can provide valuable information for future insurance and risk management applications.

---

## Actuarial Relevance

This project explores the intersection between:

- Climate Risk Modeling
- Actuarial Science
- Statistical Analysis
- Insurance Risk Management

Future extensions include integrating loss models, socioeconomic vulnerability indicators, extreme value theory, and climate scenario analysis.
